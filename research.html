Research

My research focuses on advanced materials, characterization techniques, high-temperature material behavior, degradation mechanisms, and scientific analysis using experimental and computational approaches.

Materials Characterization

Using SEM, EDS, XRD, TEM, and advanced microscopy techniques to investigate microstructure evolution, degradation behavior, surface morphology, and phase transformations in materials systems.

High-Temperature Materials

Studying material behavior under demanding environments, including oxidation, degradation, thermal stability, and long-term structural performance.

Thermodynamic Modeling

Applying computational thermodynamics and scientific modeling tools to understand phase stability, material interactions, and reaction mechanisms in complex systems.

Scientific Visualization

Developing scientific figures, animations, visual simulations, and graphical representations to communicate complex engineering concepts clearly.

Synchrotron Science

Strong interest in synchrotron radiation techniques and their applications in advanced materials characterization and analysis.

Experimental Research

Designing and conducting experimental investigations, materials testing, analytical measurements, and research workflows for engineering studies.
