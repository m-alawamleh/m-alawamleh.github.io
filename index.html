About

I am a Ph.D. researcher in Materials Engineering at the University of Alberta, with a background that began in physics and gradually moved toward materials science. I became interested in how materials behave, fail, degrade, and survive under demanding conditions.


My work combines experimental research, characterization techniques, thermodynamic modeling, and scientific analysis. I enjoy turning complicated scientific concepts into clean visual stories, whether through research figures, microscopy images, simulations, or presentations.


Outside research, I enjoy photography, travel, trains, landscapes, long walks, and scientific discussions that somehow become philosophical debates. This website is both a professional portfolio and a personal archive of research, tools, experiences, and life in Canada.
